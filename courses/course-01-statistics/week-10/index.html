Bayesian Inference

This week applies Bayes' theorem systematically to parameter estimation. Bayesian methods provide full probability distributions over parameters and natural uncertainty quantification.

Learning Objectives

  • Derive posterior distributions for common prior-likelihood combinations
  • Understand and apply conjugate priors
  • Compare MAP estimation to MLE
  • Construct and interpret credible intervals
  • Apply Bayesian methods to regression and classification

Key Concepts

  • Conjugate Prior: Prior that yields same-family posterior
  • MAP Estimation: Mode of posterior distribution
  • Credible Interval: Interval with specified posterior probability
  • Posterior Predictive: Predictions integrating over parameter uncertainty
