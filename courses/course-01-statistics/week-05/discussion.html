Week 5 Discussion Topics

Explore the philosophical and practical implications of Bayesian reasoning in AI.

Topic 1: The Base Rate Fallacy in AI Systems

Many people—and AI systems—fail to properly account for base rates when interpreting evidence.

  • Why do people intuitively expect a 95% accurate test to yield 95% certainty?
  • How might AI systems in medical diagnosis, fraud detection, or content moderation fall prey to base rate fallacies?
  • What design principles could help AI systems and their users better understand probabilistic predictions?

Topic 2: Choosing Priors—Subjectivity in Bayesian Analysis

The choice of prior is often criticized as introducing subjectivity into statistical analysis.

  • Is the subjectivity of priors a weakness or a strength of Bayesian methods?
  • When might different analysts justifiably choose different priors for the same problem?
  • How do "empirical Bayes" methods attempt to bridge Bayesian and frequentist perspectives?

Topic 3: Bayesian Updating in AI Agents

AI agents must continuously update beliefs as they interact with environments.

  • How do reinforcement learning algorithms incorporate Bayesian-like belief updating?
  • What challenges arise when an AI agent must update beliefs in real-time with streaming data?
  • How should an AI handle situations where its prior model is fundamentally wrong (model misspecification)?

Topic 4: Bayes' Theorem and Explainable AI

Bayesian models offer a path toward interpretable AI decisions.

  • How can the prior-likelihood-posterior framework help explain AI decisions to non-experts?
  • Compare the explainability of a Naive Bayes classifier to a deep neural network. What are the tradeoffs?
  • Should regulated AI systems (healthcare, finance, criminal justice) be required to use interpretable Bayesian methods?
