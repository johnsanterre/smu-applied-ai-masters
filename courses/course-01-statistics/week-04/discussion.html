Week 4 Discussion Topics

Explore how joint distributions and independence assumptions shape machine learning models.

Topic 1: The Independence Assumption in Practice

Many ML algorithms assume independence between features or observations.

  • Why does Naive Bayes often work well in practice despite its clearly violated independence assumption?
  • In what scenarios might the independence assumption lead to poor model performance?
  • How do models like Hidden Markov Models or RNNs relax independence assumptions?

Topic 2: Copulas and Dependence Modeling

Copulas separate marginal distributions from dependence structure.

  • Why might modeling dependence separately from marginals be useful in practice?
  • How are copulas used in risk modeling for finance or other domains?
  • What are the limitations of assuming Gaussian dependence (elliptical copulas)?

Topic 3: High-Dimensional Joint Distributions

In modern ML, we often deal with thousands or millions of variables.

  • Why does estimating joint distributions become intractable in high dimensions ("curse of dimensionality")?
  • How do graphical models help represent high-dimensional distributions compactly?
  • What role do variational autoencoders and normalizing flows play in learning complex joint distributions?
