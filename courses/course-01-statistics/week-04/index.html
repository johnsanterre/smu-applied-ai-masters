Joint Distributions & Independence

This week extends probability to multiple random variables simultaneously. Understanding joint distributions is essential for modeling relationships between features in machine learning.

Learning Objectives

  • Define and compute joint probability distributions for multiple variables
  • Derive marginal distributions from joint distributions
  • Calculate conditional distributions and apply them
  • Determine whether random variables are independent
  • Understand the multivariate Normal distribution

Key Concepts

  • Joint Distribution: P(X, Y) describes probability over multiple variables
  • Marginal Distribution: P(X) = Σᵧ P(X, Y) obtained by summing/integrating out variables
  • Conditional Distribution: P(Y|X) = P(X,Y)/P(X)
  • Independence: P(X, Y) = P(X)P(Y) for all x, y
  • Multivariate Normal: Extension of Gaussian to multiple dimensions
