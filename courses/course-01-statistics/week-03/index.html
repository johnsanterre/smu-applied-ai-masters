Expectation & Variance

This week introduces the fundamental summary statistics of probability distributions. Expected value and variance are ubiquitous in machine learning—from loss functions to optimization algorithms.

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate expected values for discrete and continuous random variables
  • Apply linearity of expectation to simplify complex calculations
  • Compute variance and standard deviation for common distributions
  • Understand and apply covariance and correlation
  • Use moment generating functions to derive moments

Key Concepts

  • Expected Value: The weighted average E[X] = Σ x·P(X=x)
  • Variance: Measure of spread Var(X) = E[(X-μ)²]
  • Linearity of Expectation: E[aX + bY] = aE[X] + bE[Y]
  • Covariance: Cov(X,Y) measures how X and Y vary together
  • Moments: E[Xⁿ] characterizes distribution shape
