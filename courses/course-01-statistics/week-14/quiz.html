Week 14 Quiz: Information Theory

Q1: Entropy H(X) is defined as:

  • Σ x × P(x)
  • -Σ P(x) log P(x)
  • Σ x × log P(x)
  • Σ (x - μ)²P(x)

Q2: Cross-entropy H(P,Q) relates to entropy as:

  • H(P,Q) = H(P) always
  • H(P,Q) = H(P) + H(Q)
  • H(P,Q) = H(P) + D_KL(P||Q)
  • H(P,Q) < H(P) always

Q3: KL divergence D_KL(P||Q) equals zero when:

  • P = Q (distributions are identical)
  • P and Q are independent
  • Both distributions are uniform
  • Never equals zero

Q4: Mutual information I(X;Y) measures:

  • The entropy of X times entropy of Y
  • Always negative values
  • Zero for all distributions
  • How much knowing Y reduces uncertainty about X

Q5: In classification, cross-entropy loss is used because:

  • It assumes Gaussian outputs
  • It is the negative log-likelihood for categorical outputs
  • It is equivalent to hinge loss
  • It only works for binary classification
