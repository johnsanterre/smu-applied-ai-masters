Information Theory Basics

This week introduces Shannon's information theory. Concepts like entropy and KL divergence are fundamental to understanding loss functions, compression, and representation learning.

Learning Objectives

  • Define and compute entropy for discrete distributions
  • Understand cross-entropy and its role as a loss function
  • Apply KL divergence to measure distribution differences
  • Compute mutual information between variables
  • Connect information theory to ML concepts

Key Concepts

  • Entropy: H(X) = -Σ P(x)log P(x)
  • Cross-Entropy: H(P,Q) = -Σ P(x)log Q(x)
  • KL Divergence: D_KL(P||Q) = Σ P(x)log(P(x)/Q(x))
  • Mutual Information: I(X;Y) = H(X) - H(X|Y)
