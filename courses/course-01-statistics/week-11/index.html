Markov Chains & Stochastic Processes

This week introduces Markov chains—sequences of random variables with the memoryless property. These are fundamental to MCMC methods and many ML algorithms.

Learning Objectives

  • Define Markov chains and the Markov property
  • Compute transition probabilities using transition matrices
  • Determine stationary distributions
  • Classify states and chain properties (ergodicity, irreducibility)
  • Apply Markov chains to real-world modeling problems

Key Concepts

  • Markov Property: Future depends only on present state
  • Transition Matrix: P where Pᵢⱼ = P(Xₙ₊₁=j|Xₙ=i)
  • Stationary Distribution: π where πP = π
  • Ergodicity: Unique stationary distribution exists and is limiting
