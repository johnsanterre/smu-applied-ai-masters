Foundations of Probability

This week introduces the fundamental concepts of probability theory that underpin all of statistics and machine learning. We explore how to formally describe random phenomena and assign probabilities to events.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Define and construct sample spaces for various random experiments
  • Identify and classify events as simple, compound, mutually exclusive, or exhaustive
  • Apply the three axioms of probability to verify valid probability assignments
  • Use counting principles (permutations, combinations) to calculate probabilities
  • Apply the addition rule and inclusion-exclusion principle

Key Concepts

  • Sample Space (Ω): The set of all possible outcomes of an experiment
  • Events: Subsets of the sample space representing outcomes of interest
  • Probability Axioms: Non-negativity, normalization, and additivity
  • Counting Principles: Multiplication rule, permutations, combinations
  • Set Operations: Union, intersection, complement in probability context

Topics Covered

  1. What is probability? Frequentist vs. Bayesian interpretations
  2. Defining experiments, outcomes, and sample spaces
  3. Events and event operations (union, intersection, complement)
  4. Kolmogorov's probability axioms
  5. Properties derived from axioms (complement rule, addition rule)
  6. Counting: multiplication principle, permutations, combinations
  7. Computing probabilities in equally likely sample spaces
