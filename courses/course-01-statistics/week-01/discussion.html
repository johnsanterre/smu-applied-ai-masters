Week 1 Discussion Topics

Engage with these topics to deepen your understanding of probability foundations and their applications in AI and machine learning.

Topic 1: Frequentist vs. Bayesian Interpretations

The frequentist interpretation views probability as the long-run frequency of events in repeated experiments, while the Bayesian interpretation treats probability as a degree of belief that can be updated with evidence.

  • Consider the statement: "There is a 30% chance it will rain tomorrow." How would a frequentist interpret this? How would a Bayesian?
  • Which interpretation seems more natural for AI systems that need to make decisions under uncertainty?
  • Can you think of scenarios where one interpretation is clearly more appropriate than the other?

Topic 2: Probability in AI Decision Making

Modern AI systems, from autonomous vehicles to medical diagnosis tools, must make decisions under uncertainty using probabilistic reasoning.

  • How might an autonomous vehicle use probability theory to decide whether to proceed through a yellow light?
  • What are the ethical implications of AI systems making probabilistic decisions that affect human lives?
  • Should AI systems be required to explain their probabilistic reasoning to users?

Topic 3: The Role of Sample Spaces in Problem Formulation

Correctly defining the sample space is crucial for accurate probability calculations. Different formulations can lead to different answers.

  • Consider the classic "Two Children Problem": A family has two children. Given that at least one is a boy, what is the probability both are boys? Why does the answer depend on how you define the sample space?
  • How might ML practitioners accidentally define sample spaces incorrectly when building models, and what problems could this cause?
  • Can you think of a real-world AI application where sample space definition significantly impacts results?

Topic 4: Limitations of Classical Probability

Not all uncertain situations fit neatly into the classical probability framework.

  • How would you assign a probability to "the next major AI breakthrough will occur within 5 years"? What challenges arise?
  • Some researchers argue that "deep uncertainty" cannot be captured by probability. What might this mean, and how should AI systems handle it?
  • Discuss the difference between risk (known probability distributions) and uncertainty (unknown distributions) in the context of AI safety.
