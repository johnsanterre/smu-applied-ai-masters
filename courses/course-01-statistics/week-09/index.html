Maximum Likelihood Estimation

This week covers MLE, the most widely used method for parameter estimation. Many ML models (logistic regression, neural networks) are trained by maximizing likelihood.

Learning Objectives

  • Define and construct likelihood functions for various distributions
  • Apply calculus to find maximum likelihood estimates
  • Understand the asymptotic properties of MLEs (consistency, normality, efficiency)
  • Compute and interpret Fisher information
  • Connect MLE to loss functions in machine learning

Key Concepts

  • Likelihood: L(θ) = P(data|θ), probability of data given parameters
  • Log-likelihood: ℓ(θ) = log L(θ), easier to maximize
  • MLE: θ̂ = argmax L(θ)
  • Fisher Information: Measures information about θ in data
  • Cramér-Rao Bound: Lower bound on estimator variance
