Week 9 Quiz: Maximum Likelihood Estimation

Q1: Why use log-likelihood instead of likelihood?

  • Log converts products to quotients
  • Log converts products to sums, easier to optimize
  • Log changes the maximum location
  • Log-likelihood is always larger

Q2: For Normal(μ, σ²) data, MLE of μ is:

  • Sample variance
  • Sample median
  • Sample mean
  • Maximum observation

Q3: Fisher information I(θ) relates to MLE variance via:

  • Higher I means higher variance
  • I determines bias
  • Var = I exactly
  • Var ≥ 1/I (Cramér-Rao bound)

Q4: Minimizing cross-entropy in logistic regression equals:

  • Maximizing Bernoulli likelihood
  • Maximizing Normal likelihood
  • Minimizing hinge loss
  • Minimizing likelihood

Q5: MLEs have which asymptotic properties?

  • Always unbiased
  • Consistent, asymptotically normal and efficient
  • Uniformly minimum variance
  • Best small-sample properties
