Course 3: Classical Artificial Intelligence: Search and Logic

Course Description

This course explores the foundational techniques of artificial intelligence developed from the 1950s through 1990s. Students learn search algorithms, logical reasoning, constraint satisfaction, and planning—techniques that remain essential for modern AI systems and provide context for understanding contemporary approaches.

Prerequisites

  • Course 1: Statistical Inference and Probabilistic Modeling
  • Course 2: Introduction to Programming and Computational Thinking

Learning Outcomes

  • Implement and analyze uninformed and informed search algorithms
  • Design admissible and consistent heuristics
  • Represent knowledge using propositional and first-order logic
  • Solve constraint satisfaction problems
  • Create planning systems for goal-oriented agents
  • Evaluate classical AI techniques for modern applications

Weekly Schedule

Week Topic
1 Introduction to AI & Intelligent Agents
2 Problem Formulation & State Spaces
3 Uninformed Search Strategies
4 Informed Search & Heuristics
5 A* and Beyond: Advanced Search
6 Local Search & Optimization
7 Adversarial Search & Game Theory
8 Constraint Satisfaction Problems
9 Propositional Logic & Inference
10 First-Order Logic
11 Knowledge Representation
12 Classical Planning
13 Planning Under Uncertainty
14 From Classical to Modern AI
15 Capstone: AI Agent Implementation

Assessment Structure

  • Programming Assignments (40%): Implement search, CSP, and planning algorithms
  • Quizzes (15%): Weekly concept checks
  • Midterm Exam (15%): Search and heuristics
  • Capstone Project (25%): Build an intelligent agent
  • Participation (5%): Discussions and peer review

Required Materials

  • Primary Text: "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach" (4th Ed.) by Russell & Norvig
  • Software: Python 3.11+, AIMA Python library
